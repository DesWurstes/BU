Local storage

To make this site work properly, we sometimes place small data files in the "local storage" on your device. What is local storage? A local storage element is a small text file that a website saves on your computer or mobile device when you visit the site. It enables the website to remember your actions and preferences (such as login, language, font size and other display preferences) over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re-entering them whenever you come back to the site or browse from one page to another.

How do we use local storage?

We do not use local storage for any purposes, except for functional purposes. These are the cookies this website keeps:
  • Time of accepting this notice and terms of use: So that you don't see those multiple times
  • The private key and the UTXO of your last upload: So that you don't lose your funds

How to control local storage

You can control and/or delete local storage as you wish – for details, see aboutcookies.org. You can delete all local storage data that are already on your computer and you can set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. If you do this, however, you may have to manually adjust some preferences every time you visit a site and some services and functionalities may not work. This website will keep your local storage empty until your click the "Accept" button below, but you won't be able to use some parts of this website.
